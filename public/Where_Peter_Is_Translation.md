# "Where Peter Is, There Is the Church"
## Homily of Bishop Roy on the Imperfect General Council

**Original Sermon in English. French Translation: *La Contrerévolution en marche***

In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost. Amen.

During the Christmas season, I sent you a link to a story I wrote for bishops and priests to encourage them to consider the present situation of the Church. As we enter this new year, I think it is very important that we all reflect on these questions.

So, this story was written in the form of a novella. Therefore, it was perhaps not as clear as it should have been for some of you. That is why I thought that today, on this first day of the year, I would address some of these subjects.

### Our Identity as Roman Catholics

You know that we are Catholics. We are Roman Catholics. That is our true name. We believe that the See of Peter is the center of our Catholic faith, the rock upon which the Church of our Lord Jesus Christ was established. There is no other rock that has been given to the Church than the rock of Peter.

Nevertheless, we are witnesses today to something terrible: the fact that this rock is under siege, that the persons occupying the throne of Peter are in reality destroyers of the Church. And, whether we like it or not, this has been the case for 70 years, almost 70 years. We have gone from destruction to destruction.

### Successive Destructions

They first attacked the faith during the "council" Vatican II by changing the holy doctrine of the Church. They attacked the sacraments of the Church one after another, making many of them very likely invalid, particularly the rite of episcopal consecration.

They attacked the Mass. They destroyed the Mass, as far as it could be destroyed. The Mass was preserved by the grace of God, but they destroyed public worship, that which is recognized everywhere as being the Catholic liturgy. They destroyed the Catholic Mass. To such an extent that Bishop Lefebvre spoke of Luther's Mass. When he referred to the new Mass, he spoke of Luther's Mass. That is to say, a Protestant mass.

This does not mean that those who attended this mass for many years did not receive blessings and consolations from God, for God is all-powerful. God can give His grace to all souls of good will, no matter how much they may have been deceived by the new religion. Nevertheless, we observe that it is a complete destruction.

We also see that they have destroyed Canon Law. They have destroyed the sacrament of marriage, the holy sacrament of marriage, by granting insane declarations of nullity to anyone who wants one. "You want to have your marriage annulled? It will be done. Pay the required sum and we will declare your marriage null and void." And so many people have remarried after that. But, in reality, these were adulteries rather than true Catholic marriages.

So this situation continues. Very soon it will be 70 years that this situation has lasted. And the question I wanted to ask my brother bishops and priests is this: how much longer? How long will this situation last?

### The Scattering of the Flock

Faced with such an apostasy, as we can easily understand, the flock of Christ has been scattered. "I will strike the shepherd and the flock will be scattered." And that is what happened.

Here and there, people resisted the revolution of Vatican II, the revolution of the new church. But there was no unity. It was rather every man for himself, and that is still the case today; we are all quite scattered. There is no bond of faith to gather us in unity.

Faced with such destruction, there have been different reactions. Some said: we must save what is most urgent, that is to say, we must save the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass. This was the reaction of Bishop Lefebvre, to whom we owe much, who maintained the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass. He was almost the only one along with Bishop de Castro Mayer at the beginning. They ensured, as much as it was in their power, that the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass as established by our Lord Jesus Christ continued, as well as the holy priesthood.

We must also mention here Bishop Thuc who also did much for the Church by consecrating bishops while being slandered for his holy actions aimed at saving the holy episcopate and the Church.

### Proposed Solutions

A few bishops therefore reacted to all this. But obviously, everyone looks toward the future and asks the question: what is the solution to this crisis in the Church? What is going to happen? How long are we going to be under the yoke of these impostors who are destroying the Church of our Lord Jesus Christ?

Various solutions have been proposed to resolve this crisis. How are we going to get out of this state of total destruction in which we are today?

Most people, and you know it, most of your friends, most of your family members, when they think of the Catholic Church, actually think of the Novus Ordo. In reality, they think of a false religion that occupies the buildings of the Catholic Church. That is what they see. They hear Francis—well, Francis, not long ago, denying the first commandment of our Lord Jesus Christ. They see the Vatican blessing homosexual unions publicly, accepting in one way or another the LGBT agenda, etc., etc.

And they are very scandalized by what they think is the Catholic Church. And for them, it is proof, if they needed proof, that this church cannot be the true Church established by our Divine Savior, our Lord Jesus Christ. And indeed, it is not. It is the Novus Ordo; it is another religion than the religion established by Christ.

#### First Solution: Waiting for Divine Intervention

So some people have said: we must wait for divine intervention. Some people have been waiting for this for 70 years, and I agree that divine intervention is necessary. The situation is so disastrous that divine intervention is necessary in a certain way.

When a certain lady was a young teenager, a young child—it was in the 60s—she told me that in her house, sometimes, they slept in ski gear because they were waiting for the three days of darkness to arrive. It was about to happen. They had to be ready to flee to the mountains or somewhere else, you know. But regardless, they had a plan to escape any punishment and the great events that would occur in the world. She is now almost 70 years old and she is no longer waiting for that.

But even if some of these things could happen, what I am trying to say here is that never, in the Church, have we waited indefinitely for God to intervene directly and settle the problems of the Church.

There was a time, as you know, the Great Western Schism, with three popes—a disaster. There was no solution. How could we have ever reconciled three different popes who had each appointed their own cardinals, who had excommunicated each other? The situation was disastrous. Nevertheless, Christians did not wait indefinitely.

At a certain point, what we call an imperfect general council was organized in the Church, including clergy from various factions, and people coming from different groups. They gathered and finally the situation of the Church was settled by the election of Martin V. The three doubtful popes resigned. Martin V was elected in their place and the unity of the Church was restored.

#### Second Solution: The Thesis of Bishop Guérard des Lauriers

Other people have developed a very complicated explanation of the situation of the Church. This is Bishop Guérard des Lauriers. You may have heard his name. Bishop Guérard des Lauriers tried to explain the situation of the Church. How is it possible that what we have before our eyes is happening?

Bishop Guérard des Lauriers was an intelligent man. He was a professor at the seminary of Écône, of Bishop Lefebvre. He was a professor and he was considered by Bishop Lefebvre and all the clergy under his direction as a very intelligent man. And before that, he held very important positions in the Catholic universities of Rome. In fact, the books we use with our seminarians here, some of them bear the *Nihil Obstat* of Father Guérard des Lauriers, who approved philosophy books, theology books for the Catholic Church, very important books that are used by many today for the formation of their priests.

So Father Guérard was an intelligent man. Nevertheless, he developed a complicated explanation saying that these people had been elected legally, therefore according to the law, but nevertheless did not have the authority of our Lord Jesus Christ to govern the Church because they were modernists and were not Catholics, in reality.

And Bishop Guérard des Lauriers said: "My explanation will disappear with time. One day, we will have to consider this church, this new church, as being a completely different church from the Catholic Church, because these men who are not true popes will appoint cardinals who will not be true cardinals and will therefore have no power of any kind to elect a Catholic pope."

I do not want to go too deeply into theology today. But I want you to know that this is another explanation that has been given, and the people who follow this explanation say today that the only solution to the crisis of the Church today will come from the conversion of certain modernists. Some in the Vatican must convert. They must gather in a council and they must drive out the impostors, the modernists.

But this solution that is proposed is a solution that puts the salvation of the Church... that puts the solution to this crisis in the hands of heretics, in the hands of those who do not have the Catholic faith. And that has never happened in the history of the Church. It was never the heretics who brought a solution to the various crises of the Church. It was always the small remnant of the Catholic faithful who, by the grace of God, with the help of God, brought a solution to the crisis in the Church.

#### Third Solution: The Imperfect General Council

And that is where the third solution comes in, which is a solution that was defended by Saint Robert Bellarmine and many other theologians before Vatican II. If you look at the theologians before Vatican II, try to find a single one among all the saints—Saint Robert Bellarmine, Saint Alphonsus Liguori and many other theologians before Vatican II—try to find a single one who explained that when the Church is in crisis, when there is a problem with the one who claims to be the pope, whether he be a doubtful pope of whom we do not know exactly if he is a true pope, or whether he be suspected of heresy as can sometimes happen, never did the theologians before Vatican II say that in this case, there is only one solution which is direct divine intervention: God must descend from heaven and settle our problems.

Never did any of them say that the thesis of Bishop Guérard des Lauriers was a solution, that we had to wait for the conversion of heretics so that the Church could be set back on its feet.

But many of them said: what must be done in such a situation is that the Church convoke an imperfect general council. What is an imperfect general council? An imperfect general council is a council without the pope. Usually, a council cannot take place without the pope. You need the pope to have a true council.

Well, an imperfect general council, such as there have been in the history of the Church, is a gathering of Catholic bishops precisely to deal with a problem occurring at the head of the Church. This is what they did at the time of the Council of Constance when there were three different popes. Bishops from all over the world gathered. It was easier at that time because there was an emperor who brought them together and forced them to unite, forced them to move forward and find a solution to the crisis of the Church.

Today, the situation is worse because not only is there a crisis in the Church, but in the world, there are no longer any Catholic kings. There is no longer a Catholic emperor. But nevertheless, an imperfect general council is a gathering of all the bishops and priests and superiors of religious orders, you know, all the influential people of the clergy in the world to deal with the situation of the Church.

And I believe that this is the solution that must come in the Church in one way or another. One day or another, the Church will have to gather and will have to remedy this situation.

### The Situation of Sedevacantism

We think that our time is a time of *Sede vacante*. That is to say, a time when there is no one to lead us in the faith. If you believe that Leo is the pope, well, does he really teach us the faith? Does he lead the Church or does he lead the Church away from the Catholic faith? Well, I think the answer is very easy, very easy to see: we are not under his direction and none of those who keep the Catholic faith today, pure and unsullied, want to be under the yoke of such a person who is a pure product of modernism.

Yes, indeed, we have no other choice than to recognize the situation of the Church. But for us, you know, this situation of a vacant See is not something that can last forever. We refuse to be part of any group that says there is no pope and there is no problem with that. You know, let us continue like this for another 70 years until, you know, either God descends from heaven to do something, or until they finally convert after having built a completely new body of doctrine, a new sacramental life, a new code of canon law, a new legal system.

After having built this completely new religion and imposed it on everyone, we will remain with these people on the side of the road. It is as if you were on a bus—well, there is a problem with the bus. The bus stops on the side of the road and everyone gets off and we decide to set up a camp on the side of the road where we gather, and in fact 70 years later, we are still in this camp. We make campfires every night. We have our beautiful liturgies and we are quite comfortable in the end with this situation. You know, it is a caricature obviously, but we believe, we believe that the Church must work toward the organization of an imperfect general council.

Something must be done. Something must be done to remedy the situation of the Church. And salvation has always come from the clergy who remained faithful in the Church; and, as far as we can know, it is once again from the Catholic clergy that it must come.

But if in the meantime, while we are working in this direction, God intervenes, God descends from heaven and takes care of the situation, everyone will be happy. But the Church cannot count and has never counted in the past on direct divine intervention to settle the problems of the Church.

### The Spirit of My Letter

This was therefore the spirit of my letter. I wanted to explain it better to you: I believe and I agree with this position which was in my heart for many years... leading our souls toward heaven, are we truly *ubi Petrus*, are we truly where Peter is?

But you will tell me: well, it's impossible, everyone is divided, there are small groups everywhere, and I agree with that. And in the past, there have been people who gathered, sometimes three, four people, they gathered and they elected a pope. Well, you know, it was a "pope," in quotation marks, because he was not the true pope. There is one who is famous in the United States, he was elected by his grandmother, I believe, and one of his brothers, and he became "Pope" Michael I. Ridiculous, absolutely ridiculous. And that is not what I am trying to tell you today. Absolutely not.

### What an Imperfect General Council Is

This imperfect general council of the Church, what is it? It is a general gathering of all those who have kept the Catholic faith. A general gathering of all those who have kept the Catholic faith. This will require, as I tell you, this will require divine intervention. This will require God to bring us together, obviously. But it will first require that Catholic bishops and priests begin to talk about this, begin to mention this and begin to tell people that yes, indeed, a solution can be found.

The solution can be found and it is not to be expected from the modernists, and it is also not the practice of the Church to wait for direct divine intervention.

Bishop Viganò wrote something on the occasion of Christmas, and in this writing, he said that one of the objectives of the globalists, one of the objectives of the enemies of God, is to make us believe that no solution can be found to our problems. Whether at the level of the State, whether at the level of the Church, whether at the level of the world in general, their intention is to bring us to the situation where we will lose all hope, all human hope, and where we will no longer have any will to walk in the right direction.

I am not in charge of the State. I am also not in charge of the Church as a whole. But as a Catholic bishop, and I have been surprised over the last few months—this was obviously happening in the background for many months—I have been surprised in discussing with various members of the clergy to see with what ease the clergy also arrived at this conclusion. But this has been for many years a taboo subject, something you are not allowed to talk about. You know, as soon as you mention that the solution could come from the gathering of all those who have kept the true faith, all those who have been faithful to the Catholic faith, you become someone who wants to elect a guy in his garage, and you know, people laugh at you, they ridicule you.

But what we know from the theologians of the past, what we know, is that yes, indeed, if the whole Church, or at least moral unity, a large gathering of clergy in the world met, considered the situation of the Church and said: we are in a situation of vacancy of the See of Peter, we must do something about it, well, this assembly would be legitimate. This assembly would be gathered in the Holy Spirit.

### My Message for This New Year

So I am speaking to you about this at the beginning of this year, firstly to explain the letter I sent or the link to the letter I sent, but also to tell you that I believe that in the years to come, we will have to start talking about this. Do not be surprised if you hear this from the pulpit. Do not be surprised if you see other members of the clergy, because with the grace of God, you know, we are not alone. There are a certain number of people in fact who have been silent but who truly believe that this must be done, who are going to speak out in this sense saying: we are the true Church of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Because at the end of the day, that is the question. If you believe that the solution must come from the modernists, then you are telling me that the modernists represent our Lord Jesus Christ. They are the ones who represent the authority of our Lord Jesus Christ on earth. And that is impossible, because to represent our Lord Jesus Christ on earth, you must have the apostolic faith, the faith of the apostles, not the "faith" of Vatican II, not the "faith" of humanism, not the "faith" of naturalism and indifferentism and all the false doctrines that have been condemned by the popes of the past.

### Our Prayer Intention for 2026

Today, we begin a new year. We begin the year 2026. It is very likely that there will be no solution in 2026. But I invite you, all of you, to unite in prayer. We must pray for our Catholic clergy. All of us, the Catholic clergy, especially the priests, the bishops, you know, especially the bishops, will have to give an account one day to Almighty God. What did you do for the Church of our Lord Jesus Christ? Did you stay in the camp on the side of the road? Did you light your campfires every night? Did you have your beautiful liturgy, did you have your beautiful parish, but did you have no concern for the state of the Church, no concern for the general situation of the Church and for souls in general who are going to hell by the thousands because they are deceived by a false hierarchy, by a false light that shines in the world according to Our Lady of La Salette?

What can you do? What can you do? Well, you can pray. You can pray. You can unite in prayer. The entire Church must unite in prayer so that Peter may be restored to the Catholic Church.

### The Feast of Saint Peter in Chains

We have this in the feast of Saint Peter in Chains. The feast of Saint Peter in Chains, I think it is celebrated at the beginning of August, during the month of August, for sure. Saint Peter in Chains is also the name of our chapel in the north. And, you know, with the grace of God, I wanted to name this chapel after Saint Peter in Chains precisely to represent the situation of the papacy.

You know that at a certain point in his life as Roman Pontiff, Saint Peter was taken away and put in prison, and he was nowhere to be found, and the whole Church was praying. The whole Church, we read in the Acts of the Apostles, the whole Church was praying so that Peter would be back in their midst. And it was then that an angel, angels came and broke Peter's chains and brought Peter back to the heart of the Church, and he knocked at the door, and the people could not believe when they opened the door, they could not believe that they were standing before Peter.

And I think that is a good representation of our situation today. How many of us pray for a Roman Pontiff? How many of us? Have we lost the sense that the Church is built on Peter? Are we satisfied with having perhaps a bishop, with having priests? Have we lost the sense of what the Catholic Church is? How often do we pray for a Roman Pontiff to be given to the Church?

### The Question of Unity

You see, this is a question that many people have been asking for many years: we cannot do this, an imperfect general council, because we are all divided. We are all divided among ourselves. So, how can we gather in a general council?

The question is therefore: must we unite so that we can have a pope? Must we have perfect unity so that we can eventually obtain a pope, or is it a pope who is going to give us this perfect unity? These are two completely different perspectives.

You know, we do not all need to agree. We, the clergy and the various parishes and various bishops across the world, do not need to agree on everything. We must agree on one thing: at this moment, the See of Peter is vacant and something must be done by the authorities of the Church. No matter how scattered they may be at this moment, something must be done.

Imagine that one day, by the grace of God—and it will happen, the Church is eternal, the Church has the words of eternal life—imagine the day, I should rather say, imagine the day when a Roman Pontiff, a true Roman Pontiff, a Catholic man elected to the papacy by the Church will be given to us.

Well, there will no longer be divisions over anything. Should we follow this or that liturgy? He will decide. Is this bishop valid or not? He will decide. Who is in charge of this or that place? He will decide. Everything will be decided by the Roman Pontiff.

You see, this idea according to which we must have perfect unity within the scattered flock before anything can be done to remedy this situation of the Church, I think this idea is false.

The reality, the truth, is that the whole Church must begin to wake up, and especially the clergy, realize what is happening, realize to what extent this situation is harmful to the Catholic Church. Try to convince other members of the clergy. Try to convince other bishops, other priests, other members of the clergy and also the laity that something must be done.

And then, with the grace of God, at a certain point—will it be in five years, in 10 years, in 15 years? I do not know, you know—but something must be done with the grace of God.

We will therefore keep this intention in our prayers. This will be, I suggest that this be our intention as a parish for this year. I pray that the Catholic clergy in the future, when God's time has come, that all the clergy of the Catholic Church, or at least the major part of it, gather by agreeing on the fact that Peter is not there and that where Peter is, there is the Church.

### An Unpopular but Necessary Message

I tell you this to tell you that it is not very popular to say what I am telling you today. People will not throw flowers at you and it will not earn you congratulations. It will be persecution. If you speak out in this sense, it will earn you persecution, that is obvious. Why? Because precisely, it is perhaps the solution that the devil wants to prevent. He would prefer that everyone remain comfortably installed in their position, that we develop, that we grow our parishes and that we are not in contact with each other, that we do not give the world this unity that God established for His Church.

The Church: one, holy, catholic and apostolic. Not "several," holy, catholic and apostolic, but ONE, holy, catholic and apostolic. We know that this unity is founded on Peter.

Let us therefore all pray together during this new year, for that is the main one of our problems. We have problems in our families. We have problems in our country. There are problems in the Church. There are problems in our parishes, etc. Well, that is the main problem. You want to identify the main problem we are facing today in the world? It is that problem. We do not have the voice of Peter. The voice of Peter is not heard in the world.

And this has been going on for a number of years. It is therefore toward this that our prayer must be directed. We must pray in this direction. You should pray that your bishops and your priests also find the courage to speak out, you know, to speak out against this false church and to say: "No, there is no question of us waiting for these people to deal with the problems of the Church. They will only make things worse."

Every time they elect one of their antipopes, one of their false popes, he is worse than the one before. He preaches the same false doctrines and pushes further the agenda of the modernists and the satanists, in fact.

### The Scandalous Example of the German Christmas Mass

On public channels, and I will finish with this today, on German "Catholic" public television channels, during the Christmas period, they broadcast a mass, a mass that was said in one of the churches in Germany. And that is the one they chose to be broadcast on a public television channel for Catholics who stay at home and cannot attend mass.

And during this mass, there was a pile of hay in the sanctuary. And I am very sorry to have to say these things before Almighty God, but we must realize what is happening. There was a pile of hay. On this pile of hay, there was an adult man in a kind of placenta, and who was crawling around, something disgusting. And he was supposed to represent the Infant Jesus, our Lord Jesus Christ.

And there were priests, German clerics. There were priests, clerics gathered around, explaining the symbolism of this horror. They said that this expresses, this brings the birth of our Lord Jesus Christ back to a common birth. In other words, they were saying: "Let us abandon this thought that our Lord Jesus Christ was such a special being."

Are these people going to be punished by the Vatican? Are they going to be reprimanded for what they have done? No. They will continue: modernism, satanism, destruction of the kingdom of our Lord Jesus Christ.

We must arrive at the conclusion that these people do not belong to the same church as us. They are a different church. They are a different church. This may not be happening in all churches for the moment, but this kind of thing, you know, is more and more frequent. The false authorities of the Church never do anything to put an end to these abuses.

But if you want to start saying the Mass in Latin, if you want to start having the true faith and the true Church, be faithful to the principles of the faith, be faithful to the Catholic liturgy, to Catholic devotions, to Catholic family life, well, you are rejected. You are driven out. You are persecuted. They make sure that you are not going to make too much noise and that you are not going to lead other people to think what you think.

### Conclusion: Our Prayer for the Return of Catholic Unity

As you see, the conclusion of all this is that we must absolutely see, hopefully in our generation, the return of Catholic unity around the See of Peter.

And that is going to be our prayer. We will pray for this intention in particular. We will pray for the priests, the bishops. We will pray so that more people arrive at this idea of saying to themselves: "Yes, perhaps God wants me to play a role in the resolution of this crisis if I am a Catholic bishop"... Who else is a Catholic bishop today? Who else? There are so few Catholic bishops who still preach the Catholic faith.

So, Catholic bishops, Catholic priests must also support their bishops in this direction, and then the laity, an army under the mantle of the Blessed Virgin Mary.

When I say, when I criticize divine intervention, do not believe that I am saying that it is going to be a human work. Absolutely not. Divine intervention passes through the efforts of the clergy and faithful Catholic priests. It is then that God will intervene.

### The Example of the Battle of Lepanto

When the Muslims invaded Europe, Pope Saint Pius V could have said: "Well, they are too numerous. We must wait for divine intervention." He did not say that. He summoned all the Catholic kings so that they would each raise their army and come to the defense of Christendom. Spain is very famous for having gathered many people and having been at the head of this fight.

And then, after the Catholic army was gathered, when it decided to fight this invasion, then God intervened.

This is our prayer today. In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost. Amen.
